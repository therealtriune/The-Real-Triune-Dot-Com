Bio

Leader, entrepreneur, and artist — a few words that define Triune. With over two decades in music and business, he's earned widespread respect and admiration, particularly across Southern California.

His path runs through multiple industries. He's toured with KRS-One, De La Soul, and Talib Kweli, produced the podcasts Worst Take The Sports Show and LabTalk, and founded UNO Marketing, his own digital marketing firm. He also launched Kicks and Cutz, Southern California's first barbershop/shoe store, and invested in The Culture Coffee Company in Las Vegas.

His music has landed in over 20 television and film placements, including the theme song for CBS Sports' The Money Fight (Mayweather vs. McGregor) and ESPN's Year of The Black QB. As an independent artist, Triune has amassed over 20 million television views, 20 million streams, and 100,000+ album sales.

His latest project, The Green Tape, is earning national recognition — the track "Wiser Den Eva" was featured in Netflix's High on the Hog, an NAACP Image Award winner for Outstanding Documentary. New music is on the way.
